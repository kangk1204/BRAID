# Ground-Truth & Orthogonally-Validated PSI Benchmark Datasets for Alternative Splicing Tools: A Comprehensive Survey

## TL;DR
- There is **no single curated "gold-standard" PSI benchmark** in the splicing field; instead, the de facto standards are (1) the RT-PCR validation panels bundled with the major tool papers — especially MAJIQ's 208 RT-PCR events (mouse cerebellum/liver + human Jurkat) and the ESRP/prostate-cancer RT-PCR panels reused in independent benchmarks — and (2) two synthetic spike-in systems, **SIRVs (Lexogen)** and **Sequins (Garvan)**, which are the only resources giving truly known, quantitative ground-truth isoform ratios.
- For experimental orthogonal validation, the field overwhelmingly relies on **RT-PCR / capillary-electrophoresis PSI** (semi-quantitative); RT-qPCR, ddPCR, splicing-sensitive microarrays (HJAY/MJAY, exon arrays), NanoString and long-read sequencing appear less often as PSI ground truth but exist in specific datasets (SEQC for junctions, LRGASP for long-read isoform quantification, MBNL microarray panels).
- The most useful resources for evaluating a new tool against ground-truth PSI are: **SIRV-Set/Sequins** (known ratios), the **MAJIQ RT-PCR compendium**, the **ESRP (GSE64357) and PC3E/GS689 prostate RT-PCR panels** (used in independent benchmarks), **VastDB/vast-tools** (huge curated PSI atlas with validation primers), **LRGASP** (long-read isoform quantification ground truth), and the **SUPPA2 TRA2 RT-PCR set** — supplemented with simulation (where ground truth is exact but synthetic).

## Key Findings

**The structural problem.** Multiple independent benchmarking groups state explicitly that experimental "gold-standard" PSI data are scarce. The Mehmood et al. (2019) and DICAST (2022) benchmarks both ultimately fell back on simulated data because real validated event sets are small and heterogeneous. Therefore a practical evaluation strategy must combine (a) synthetic spike-ins with exactly known ratios, (b) simulation with injected ground truth, and (c) the published experimental RT-PCR panels — none alone is sufficient.

**Validation-technique reality.** RT-PCR with capillary electrophoresis / fluorescent labeling (yielding a PSI from band intensities) is the field's "gold standard in the field" (MAJIQ-SPEL's own words). RT-qPCR, ddPCR, microarrays, and long reads are used but less commonly tied to quantitative PSI ground truth.

### (a) Experimentally-validated RT-PCR / qPCR panels

**MISO (Katz et al., Nature Methods 2010; 7(12):1009–1015; PMID 21057496).** Human. Cassette exons, alternative isoforms, alternative cleavage/polyadenylation; implicated hnRNP H1. Validation by RT-qPCR; estimates reported to validate at high rates. The paper notes isoform-level estimates are "harder to validate in the lab using traditional techniques such as RT-PCR." PSI estimates are produced as posterior (MCMC) distributions. The annotation/event sets (Wang et al. 2008, ~67,000 events) are widely reused. Caveat: the original RT-PCR set is modest and not the main reason MISO is cited.

**rMATS (Shen et al., PNAS 2014; 111(51):E5593–E5601; PMID 25480548).** Human. SE, A5SS, A3SS, MXE, RI. Validation by quantitative fluorescent RT-PCR in prostate cancer cell lines (PC3E vs GS689, epithelial/mesenchymal). Per the paper: "We randomly selected 34 exon skipping events for validation by quantitative fluorescent RT-PCR… These 34 exons covered a broad range of ψ values with the between-group difference |Δψ| ranging from 0.10 to 0.90," yielding the reported ~94% validation rate (an event was "validated" if average RT-PCR inclusion differed by ≥5% between three replicates with direction matching RNA-seq). This PC3E/GS689 panel (Carstens lab, originating from the Warzecha/ESRP work) became one of the most reused experimental sets; RNA-seq is at SRA **SRS354082**. The derived "HVS" set of 32 qPCR-validated DS genes comes from this lineage.

**MAJIQ (Vaquero-Garcia et al., eLife 2016; e11752).** Mouse + human. Local splicing variations (LSVs) covering all classic types plus complex/novel events. Validation by **208 RT-PCR validations** spanning two mouse tissues (cerebellum and liver, from Zhang et al. 2014) and a human Jurkat T-cell line (unstimulated/stimulated, Cole et al. 2015). Per the paper: "an extensive set of 208 RT-PCR validations… MAJIQ achieved an overall correlation of R = 0.8 and R = 0.95 for PSI and dPSI quantification by RT-PCR." This is arguably the single most-cited RT-PCR PSI compendium for tool comparison; subsequent tools (Whippet, SUPPA2, PSI-Sigma) reuse the same cerebellum/liver/Jurkat RT-PCR values. MAJIQ-SPEL (Bioinformatics 2018, 34(2):300) provides automated RT-PCR primer design for LSVs and includes worked validation examples (e.g., a novel Ptbp1 exon, Camk2d, Fubp3) in mouse cerebellum/adrenal. Caveat: for complex LSVs, an RT-PCR PSI maps to a junction fraction, not a whole-isoform fraction.

**SUPPA2 (Trincado et al., Genome Biology 2018; 19:40; PMID 29571299).** Human. Cassette exons + others. Validation against **83 cassette events** differentially spliced upon TRA2A/TRA2B double knockdown in MDA-MB-231 cells (detected by SUPPA2 for 66, rMATS 78, MAJIQ 72), plus a **44 RT-PCR negative-event set** for false-positive-rate estimation (FPR: SUPPA2 1/31, rMATS 2/35, MAJIQ 2/36, DEXSeq 2/25). Additional file 2: Table S14 contains the data and TRA2B CLIP-validated novel events (CHRAC1, NDRG3, METTL10, etc.). Useful because it bundles both positive and negative ground-truth events with directly comparable cross-tool numbers.

**Whippet (Sterne-Weiler et al., Molecular Cell 2018; S1097-2765(18)30678-6).** Mouse + human. All complexity classes including microexons and high-entropy tandem events. Reuses the mouse liver/cerebellum and human Jurkat RT-PCR PSI values (from MAJIQ/Vaquero-Garcia) plus 10 new RT-PCR-tested complex events in mouse N2a neuroblastoma cells (Raj et al. 2014 data). Event-level tools showed ~2.5-fold lower median error than transcript-level tools.

**PSI-Sigma (Lin et al., Bioinformatics 2019; PMC6901072).** Human. Single- and multiple-exon-skipping (MES), IR. Benchmarked on **RNA Sequins** (synthetic ground truth) plus **130 RT-PCR validations** for single-exon events (correlation with RT-PCR r = 0.8741). Notable for explicitly using Sequins concentration annotation as ΔPSI ground truth and comparing rMATS/SUPPA2/Whippet, and for highlighting IR and MES, which most tools handle poorly.

**vast-tools / VastDB (Tapial et al., Genome Research 2017; 27(10):1759; PMC5630039; framework: Gohr et al. 2022, PMID 35895261).** Human, mouse, chicken (+ more). All major AS types including microexons (3–15 nt). Built from **1,478 RNA-seq datasets (108 human, 139 mouse, 61 chicken sample sets)**. PSI quantifications validated at high rates by RT-PCR across many companion papers (Han 2013, Braunschweig 2014, Irimia 2014, Raj 2014, etc.). VastDB (vastdb.crg.eu) provides downloadable PSI tables across tissues and, critically, **suggested RT-PCR validation primers and expected band sizes for each event**. Events have stable IDs (e.g., HsaEX6000001). This is the largest curated quantitative PSI atlas with built-in validation design. Caveat: PSI values are RNA-seq-derived (not independently measured) except where companion RT-PCR exists; the annotation-based (ANNOT/COMBI) module is less validated than the exon-skipping modules.

**Brooks et al. (Genome Research 2011; 21(2):193–202; PMID 20921232) and Brooks et al. (Genome Research 2015; 25(11):1771–1780; PMID 26294686).** *Drosophila* (with conserved mammalian orthologs). The 2011 paper ("Conservation of an RNA regulatory map between Drosophila and mammals"; Pasilla/PS = NOVA ortholog RNAi + RNA-seq) identified **405 splicing events in 323 genes** and is the origin of the JuncBASE tool; it is frequently cited as a gold-standard exon-skipping validation reference. The 2015 paper (56 RBPs by RNAi) identified **23,079 AS events, 2,876 significantly altered**, with RT-PCR confirmation and notable coverage of alternative first exons. PSI defined via JuncBASE. Caveat: primarily *Drosophila*, so most useful for cross-species work or as methodological precedent.

**ENCODE RBP knockdown compendium (Van Nostrand et al., Nature 2020, "A Large-Scale Binding and Functional Map of Human RNA Binding Proteins").** Human (HepG2, K562). All five rMATS event types (SE, RI, A3SS, A5SS, MXE). **~250+ RBPs depleted by shRNA/CRISPR**, with paired eCLIP; rMATS output files available at the ENCODE DCC (e.g., accession **ENCSR413YAF** lists rMATS outputs; 473 RBP-KD RNA-seq datasets feature in the RBP-Maps analysis). DARTS (Zhang et al., Nature Methods) used 196 RBPs × 2 cell lines = 408 knockdown-vs-control comparisons for training. The largest perturbation RNA-seq resource with consistent rMATS PSI/ΔPSI; not RT-PCR-validated genome-wide but individual events validated in companion papers. Excellent for "does my tool recover known RBP-responsive events with the right direction" testing.

**MBNL / CELF / myotonic dystrophy panels.** Mouse + human. Extensive RT-PCR-validated cassette-exon panels. Examples: Mbnl1 ΔE3/ΔE3 KO brain on splicing-sensitive microarrays + RT-PCR validation (sepscore >0.3 validates by RT-PCR; Sorbs1, Spag9, Dclk1 events; PMC3302840 / PLOS ONE 2012); the HSA^LR^ mouse where **128/172 mis-splicing events were shared with MBNL1 KO and 33 were RT-PCR-validated**; Mbnl2 KO (Charizanis et al. 2012, PMID 22884328); and the RBFOX1/MBNL1 co-regulation panel (PMC4161394). These provide curated, RT-PCR-quantified PSI for disease-relevant exons (Clcn1 exon 7a, Atp2a1 exon 22, Nfix exon 7, Serca1 exon 11). Caveat: many use microarray skip/include ratio rather than direct PSI; events are disease-specific.

**ESRP / Warzecha epithelial splicing (GSE64357 — mouse Esrp double-KO epidermis; PC3E/GS689 human).** Used as the "MVS" (28 qPCR-validated exon-skipping genes, mouse; GEO **GSE64357**, samples GSM1569076-77/GSM1569083-84) and "HVS" (32 qPCR-validated DS genes, human prostate) sets in Mehmood et al. (2019). Directly downloadable; among the few packaged qPCR-validated event sets with clean accessions.

### (b) Synthetic spike-in ground-truth controls

**SIRVs — Spike-In RNA Variants (Lexogen; Paul et al., bioRxiv 080747, 2016).** Human-derived synthetic sequences that model alternative splicing, alternative TSS/TES, and overlapping/antisense transcripts. The isoform module contains **69 artificial transcript variants derived from 7 human model genes (KLK5, LDHD, LGALS17A, DAPK3, HAUS5, USF2, TESK2), with between 6 and 18 transcript variants each (on average 9.9 physically realized alternative isoforms)**; transcripts are 191–2,528 nt with GC 29.5–51.2%. Provided as mixes **E0, E1, E2**: "E0… all 69 transcripts are present in equimolar concentrations… E1 already contains a moderate concentration distribution… E2 represents the natural situation, whereby a dominant, abundant transcript variant is transcribed… together with (up to 17) other variants present at lower expression levels (down to <1%)," with concentration differences up to 128-fold. The relative isoform ratios (a direct proxy for ground-truth PSI) are KNOWN by design — making SIRVs the single most direct experimental ground-truth for splicing/isoform quantification. **SIRV-Set 4 (Cat. No. 141)** = 69 isoforms (7 genes) + **15 long SIRVs (3 each at 4/6/8/10/12 kb, equimolar)** + **92 mono-exonic ERCC transcripts spanning ~6 orders of magnitude** — this is the spike-in used by LRGASP. Caveat: high inter-isoform sequence similarity makes some isoforms genuinely hard to resolve (a useful stress-test); only 7 loci, so genomic diversity is limited; cumulative pipetting error <±8%.

**Sequins (Hardwick et al., Nature Methods 2016; 13:792–798; nmeth.3958; PMID 27502218).** Entirely artificial sequence mapping to an in silico chromosome. **164 isoforms across 78 genes (avg 2.1, up to 4 isoforms/gene)**. Provided in two mixtures (A and B) at different molar ratios to emulate fold-changes → known ΔPSI ground truth; spans ~10^6-fold concentration range. Data deposited in GEO (accessions in paper); resource site sequin.xyz; protocols on protocols.io. Used by PSI-Sigma and many long-read benchmarks (e.g., Dong et al. 2023 H1975/HCC827 mixtures). Caveat: Sequins map multiple features to the same molecule, so per-event PSI assignment is less clean than SIRVs (per Lexogen's comparison); fewer isoforms per gene.

**ERCC spike-ins (External RNA Control Consortium).** Single mono-exonic transcripts — NOT isoform-level, so they provide no PSI ground truth. Relevant only for dynamic-range/normalization checks; bundled in SIRV-Set 4 and SEQC.

### (c) Long-read-based ground truth

**LRGASP (Pardo-Palacios et al., Nature Methods 2024; 21(7):1349–1363; DOI 10.1038/s41592-024-02298-3; PMID 38849569).** Human + mouse + manatee. The most comprehensive long-read transcriptome benchmark. **>427 million long reads** (PacBio Sequel II, ONT MinION/PromethION, plus Illumina HiSeq short reads), four library prep methods (ONT cDNA, ENCODE PacBio cDNA, R2C2, CapTrap, plus direct RNA), ~50 pipelines. Three challenges: (1) transcript identification, (2) quantification, (3) de novo reconstruction. Ground truth came from **SIRV-Set 4 spike-ins, simulated data, and undisclosed manually-curated GENCODE transcript models**; quantification assessed with SCC, MRD, NRMSE and PET against known SIRV/simulation concentrations. Samples: human **WTC11 iPSC**, **H1-mix** (H1 ES + H1-derived definitive endoderm), a **mouse ES cell line** (Castaneus × S129/SvJae F121-9), and **manatee whole blood**.

Data availability: the bulk of raw long- and short-read data lives on the **ENCODE Portal** (search `?type=Experiment&internal_tags=LRGASP`), catalogued in Supplementary Data 1 — there is **no single GEO super-series**. Concrete example WTC11 accessions: long-read **ENCSR507JOF**, short-read **ENCSR673UKZ** (per the companion SQANTI3 paper, Nat Methods 21:793). Named orthogonal/validation accessions include GEO **GSE185917** (ssCAGE WTC11); QuantSeq **ENCSR322MWL/GSE219685** (WTC11), **ENCSR813AOB/GSE219788** (H1), **ENCSR198UNH/GSE219571** (H1-DE); and SRA RT-PCR validation reads **SRR24680098/99** and **SRR23881262**. Manatee assembly **GCA_030013775.1** (BioProject PRJNA939417). Portals: data overview at cgl.gi.ucsc.edu/data/LRGASP/ ; data matrix at cgl.gi.ucsc.edu/data/LRGASP/rnaseq-data-matrix.html ; simulation + ground truth at cgl.gi.ucsc.edu/data/LRGASP/data/simulation/ ; code at github.com/LRGASP. Caveat: LRGASP is oriented to transcript-isoform identification/quantification rather than local PSI per se — but isoform quantities convert to event PSI, and it is the modern standard for long-read validation.

**SG-NEx (Singapore Nanopore Expression project).** Human cell lines; direct RNA + cDNA ONT long reads with matched short reads and Sequins spike-ins. Useful as an orthogonal long-read cross-check for isoform/PSI and for identifying alternative isoforms, novel/fusion transcripts and m6A.

**Long reads as orthogonal validation for short-read PSI.** Several papers (FulQuant, Nat Commun 2021; Dong et al. 2023 in silico mixtures of H1975/HCC827 with Sequins) pair short- and long-read data on the same samples, providing a practical (if imperfect) orthogonal PSI comparison.

### (d) Simulation-based ground truth

Simulators (Polyester, BEERS, RSEM-sim, Flux Simulator, ASimulator) inject exactly-known PSI/ΔPSI and are used heavily because experimental ground truth is scarce:
- **Mehmood et al. (2019, Briefings in Bioinformatics; bbz126; PMC7711265):** 10 tools; used the MVS (28 mouse Esrp qPCR genes, **GSE64357**) and HVS (32 human prostate qPCR genes) real validated sets, plus HCa (**GSE77314**, 100 HCC tumor/normal samples) and the PCa set. Found weak consensus; rMATS/SUPPA2 best on high-depth/low-variability data, MAJIQ best on complex events. This is the most directly relevant prior benchmark for a new differential-splicing tool.
- **DICAST (2022, bioRxiv 2022.01.05.475067):** modular Docker/Snakemake pipeline benchmarking 11 mappers + 8 event-detection tools on simulated + whole-blood RNA-seq; STAR/HISAT2 best mappers; no event tool dominated; recommends a consensus approach.
- **Comprehensive event-level benchmark (Briefings in Bioinformatics 2023, bbad121):** 21 differential splicing tools on simulated data with injected ground truth; recommends DEXSeq and rMATS for citation/maintenance.
- **Plant benchmark (BMC Bioinformatics, PMC4271460):** 8 tools on simulated + real Arabidopsis with PCR verification.
Caveat: simulation omits real biological/technical confounders (outliers, positional bias, annotation error).

### (e) Large consortium resources

**SEQC / MAQC-III (SEQC/MAQC-III Consortium, Nature Biotechnology 2014; 32(9):903–914; nbt.2957; PMID 25150838).** Human reference RNA (UHRR = sample A, Human Brain Reference = sample B, + mixtures C/D). Examined junction discovery and DE across Illumina HiSeq 2000, SOLiD, and 454 at >10 sites. **Discovered unannotated exon-exon junctions, with >80% validated by qPCR** — directly relevant to splice-junction detection accuracy. Bundled TaqMan qPCR for ~1,000 genes (955 control genes), 20,801 PrimePCR reactions, and ERCC spike-ins. Data: GEO **GSE5350** (TaqMan/MAQC-I); full SEQC data via GEO (Data Citation 3); companion ABRF-NGS study at **GSE46876** (included long-read and semiconductor sequencers). Bioconductor `seqc` package contains 46 exon-exon junction tables + TaqMan + ERCC data. Caveat: the TaqMan ground truth is gene-level expression, not PSI; the splicing-relevant ground truth here is junction-detection validation, not quantitative event PSI.

**GTEx.** Human, ~50 tissues. Widely used for tissue-differential splicing and sQTLs (LeafCutter). Provides reproducibility/biological-replicate ground truth (consistency across tissues) but no orthogonal experimental PSI validation per se. The RBFOX1 MXE heart-vs-brain switch is a canonical visual example. Used as a "biological truth" set in tool papers.

**LeafCutter (Li et al., Nature Genetics 2018; 50:151; s41588-017-0004-9).** Human. Intron-excision (annotation-free) covering SE, A5SS, A3SS, and complex events; PSI = intron excision ratio. Benchmarked by simulation (ROC vs Cufflinks2/rMATS/MAJIQ) and GTEx tissue pairs (Testis vs Kidney, Muscle vs Colon, Heart vs Lung, Brain vs Liver). LeafCutterMD (Bioinformatics 2020) adds outlier detection for rare-disease cohorts (Mayo Clinic). No dedicated RT-PCR panel; ground truth is simulation + tissue differences.

**JuncBASE, DEXSeq, SpliceTrap, JUM.** JuncBASE (Brooks) — used in the Drosophila RBP studies above. DEXSeq (Anders) — exon-bin based, recommended in benchmarks for maintenance/citation. SpliceTrap (Wu et al.) — intron-centric, IR focus, validated against exon arrays. JUM (Wang & Rio) — splice-graph based. These are methodological references rather than ground-truth datasets. Splicing-sensitive microarray data referenced as orthogonal ground truth include the **HJAY** array (RASA paper repository) and **HTA-2.0** (e.g., A549 ± SRSF1 siRNA at GEO **GSE76902**), plus the Affymetrix Human Exon 1.0 ST in cancer (RT-PCR-validated).

## Details: De Facto "Gold Standards"

A new tool would be expected to evaluate against, at minimum:
1. **A synthetic spike-in with known ratios** — SIRVs (or Sequins) — the only source of truly known ground truth. Increasingly expected since ~2018.
2. **The MAJIQ RT-PCR compendium** (mouse cerebellum/liver + human Jurkat, 208 events) — the most reused experimental RT-PCR PSI set; reusing it places your tool on the same plot as MAJIQ/Whippet/SUPPA2.
3. **The ESRP/prostate (PC3E/GS689; SRS354082) and Esrp-KO (GSE64357) qPCR sets** — packaged, downloadable, used by the independent Mehmood et al. benchmark.
4. **Simulation** with injected ΔPSI — for controlled sensitivity/precision/FPR curves across depth and replicate number.
5. **LRGASP / long-read orthogonal data + SIRV-Set 4** — now expected if the tool touches isoform-level or long-read quantification.

## Recommendations

**Staged evaluation plan:**

**Stage 1 — Exact ground truth (do first).** Run on **SIRV mixes E0/E1/E2** (Lexogen) and/or **Sequins mixes A/B** (sequin.xyz). These give known isoform ratios → compute correlation of your PSI and ΔPSI vs design values. Target benchmark: Pearson R > 0.9 for ΔPSI on SIRV E1/E2, matching PSI-Sigma's reported performance. If your tool cannot recover known SIRV ratios, fix before proceeding.

**Stage 2 — Reused experimental RT-PCR PSI.** Download the **MAJIQ RT-PCR validation values** (eLife 2016 supplements: mouse cerebellum/liver + Jurkat, 208 events) and the **SUPPA2 TRA2 set** (83 positive + 44 negative events; Genome Biology 2018, Additional file 2 Table S14). Reproduce the standard |ΔPSI_predicted − ΔPSI_RTPCR| cumulative-error plot and the FPR-on-negatives. Target: median error and FPR at or below rMATS/MAJIQ/SUPPA2 on the identical events. This places your tool directly against incumbents.

**Stage 3 — Packaged qPCR-validated differential-splicing sets.** Use **GSE64357 (mouse Esrp KO, 28 qPCR genes)** and the **PC3E/GS689 prostate panel (HVS, 32 genes; SRA SRS354082)** exactly as Mehmood et al. (2019) did. Report proportion of qPCR-validated DS genes recovered, and add the rMATS 34-event PC3E/GS689 set (Δψ 0.10–0.90) for a quantitative correlation.

**Stage 4 — Scale & perturbation recovery.** Use the **ENCODE RBP-KD compendium** (HepG2/K562; rMATS outputs at the ENCODE DCC) to test whether your tool recovers known RBP-responsive events and concordant ΔPSI directions across hundreds of comparisons. Use **VastDB** to cross-check PSI magnitudes against a curated atlas and to obtain RT-PCR primers/band sizes if you run your own wet-lab validation.

**Stage 5 — Simulation for controlled curves.** Generate Polyester/RSEM-sim data with injected ΔPSI across depths (e.g., 20–100M reads) and replicate counts (2–6) to produce precision/recall/FPR/ROC curves — the only way to get clean ROC with large positive sets.

**Stage 6 (if long-read or isoform-level).** Evaluate on **LRGASP** WTC11 and mouse-ES data and **SIRV-Set 4**, using the LRGASP metrics (SCC, MRD, NRMSE, PET). Assemble the data from the ENCODE Portal (`internal_tags=LRGASP`).

**Event-type coverage.** Test each type explicitly. SE/MXE/A5SS/A3SS are well covered by MAJIQ/SUPPA2/SIRV sets; **IR is under-represented** — supplement with PSI-Sigma's IR Sequins analysis and dedicated IR datasets (intron-retention cancer transcriptomes; Y14/EJC-knockdown IR RT-PCR sets). **Alternative first/last exons** are poorly covered by most RT-PCR panels — VastDB and the Brooks *Drosophila* first-exon data are the main resources.

**Thresholds that would change the recommendation.** If your tool is long-read/isoform-resolution, prioritize LRGASP + Sequins + SIRV-Set 4 over short-read RT-PCR panels. If it targets clinical/rare-disease outlier splicing, prioritize LeafCutterMD-style cohorts and MBNL/DMD disease panels over spike-ins. If it targets IR specifically, weight Sequins/PSI-Sigma IR and dedicated IR RT-PCR sets most heavily.

## Caveats
- **"Validation rates" are not PSI accuracy.** Tool papers often report % of events "validated" (direction confirmed) rather than quantitative PSI correlation; treat the 94% (rMATS), high MISO rates, and similar figures as directional, not quantitative.
- **RT-PCR PSI is itself an estimate** with its own biases (amplification efficiency, heteroduplexes, primer design); it is "orthogonal" but not absolute truth. ddPCR and capillary electrophoresis reduce but do not eliminate this.
- **Complex/LSV events:** a single RT-PCR PSI may not map cleanly to a tool's whole-isoform PSI; MAJIQ explicitly notes PSI captures junction fractions, not whole-exon fractions.
- **Spike-ins lack genomic context** (7 SIRV loci; 78 Sequin genes) and do not capture real chromatin/sequence diversity, so good SIRV performance ≠ good genome-wide performance.
- **Reuse caution:** many "independent" validations recycle the same cerebellum/liver/Jurkat RT-PCR values, so cross-paper agreement can be partly circular.
- **Accession fragmentation:** LRGASP raw data is spread across many ENCODE experiment accessions, not one series; budget time to assemble it from Supplementary Data 1 / the ENCODE Portal.
- **Simulation ≠ reality:** injected ground truth omits real confounders; never rely on simulation alone.
- **Coverage gap I could not close:** within the available searches I could not confirm a dedicated, widely-used **NanoString or ddPCR splicing-PSI benchmark dataset** packaged specifically for tool evaluation; these techniques appear in individual studies but not as a standard, reusable benchmark resource. Similarly, GTEx and SEQC provide reproducibility/junction-validation truth rather than quantitative event-level PSI ground truth.
