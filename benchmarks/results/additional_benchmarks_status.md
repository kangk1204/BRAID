## Differential model ablation: pooled `sum` vs replicate-aware `rep`

All three head-to-head datasets (TRA2/GSE59335, Circadian/GSE54651, SRS354082/PC3E-GS689)
carry 3-vs-3 rMATS **per-replicate** count vectors, so both differential models run on
every matched event — there is no single-replicate two-group dataset in the head-to-head
set. The ablation feeds each model's ΔPSI posterior through the **same** leakage-free
cross-fit conformal procedure and scores against the same RT-PCR truth:

- `sum` — pooled Jeffreys-Beta on length-normalized summed group counts
  (`braid differential --differential-model sum`; the manuscript's BRAID-Jeffreys point).
- `rep` — resample the per-replicate count vectors, draw a per-replicate Beta PSI, average
  within group (`--differential-model rep`, and what `auto` selects per-event when ≥2
  complete informative replicate pairs exist in both groups).

Result (n=196, `n_fell_back_to_sum: 0` so `rep` ran on every event): the two models are
**statistically indistinguishable** on RT-PCR coverage (paired McNemar p=0.48); both reach
nominal 95% coverage, and per-dataset they tie on Circadian and SRS354082. This is why the
manuscript's pooled-`sum` head-to-head numbers are unaffected by the CLI's `auto`/`rep`
default. Artifact `benchmarks/results/sum_vs_rep_eval.json`; regenerate with
`python benchmarks/headtohead/sum_vs_rep_eval.py`.

## Artifact Provenance (canonical vs scratch)

The JSON files in this tracked `benchmarks/results/` directory are the **canonical,
reviewer-facing** snapshots, each regenerated by the matching driver under `benchmarks/`:

| Artifact | Canonical for | Regenerated by |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| `headtohead_coverage.json` | **head-to-head ΔPSI coverage** (4 methods × 3 datasets; common n=139, rMATS-matched n=196; McNemar) — the manuscript headline | `benchmarks/headtohead/comprehensive_benchmark.py` |
| `recalibration_ablation.json` | recalibration-step ablation (same conformal on every method's point estimate) | `benchmarks/headtohead/recalibration_ablation.py` |
| `adaptive_conformal_eval.json` | interval-width irreducibility (adaptive conformal does not sharpen) | `benchmarks/headtohead/adaptive_conformal_eval.py` |
| `betabinom_dispersion_eval.json` | formal Beta-Binomial dispersion vs heuristic λ | `benchmarks/headtohead/betabinom_dispersion_eval.py` |
| `rtpcr_benchmark.json` | PacBio A3SS/A5SS non-SE coverage (252-event reproduction) | `benchmarks/rtpcr_benchmark.py` |
| `pacbio_sharpness.json` | PacBio sharpness vs matched-width random baseline | `benchmarks/scripts/pacbio_sharpness_analysis.py` |
| `sum_vs_rep_eval.json` | **differential-model ablation** — pooled `sum` vs replicate-aware `rep` ΔPSI posterior through the same cross-fit conformal (supplementary; the manuscript head-to-head uses pooled `sum`) | `benchmarks/headtohead/sum_vs_rep_eval.py` |
| `bin_policy_eval.json` | support-bin (Mondrian) policy ablation for conformal calibration (supplementary) | `benchmarks/headtohead/bin_policy_eval.py` |
| `comprehensive_benchmark.json` | **legacy transcript isoform-recovery (assembler) precision** (K562/GM12878/IMR90 PR-curves) — NOT the head-to-head, and NOT cited by the manuscript | legacy decomposer/multi-dataset isoform driver |
| `qki_rmats_benchmark_results.json` | legacy QKI RT-PCR SE detection — NOT cited by the manuscript (QKI section was removed) | `benchmarks/qki_rmats_benchmark.py` |

Notes:
- Numeric fields are scoped to their event: e.g. `effective_scale ≈ 0.0096` in
  `rtpcr_benchmark.json` is the per-event posterior count scale, **not** a
  transcript-recovery sensitivity — those are distinct metrics in distinct artifacts.
- The untracked, local `benchmark_results/` (singular) directory is scratch output from
  ad-hoc full runs; it is gitignored and **not authoritative**. Ignore any stale numbers
  there in favour of the canonical artifacts above.
